Why NOT Max RRSP? The RRIF Trap.

At $31.5K/yr for 20 years, your RRSP hits ~$1.37M. At age 72, you're forced to withdraw ~$72K+/year (RRIF minimums). Combined with CPP and holdco income, you're in the 48%+ bracket AND lose OAS entirely (clawback starts at ~$93K income).

At $18K/yr, the RRSP reaches ~$783K. RRIF withdrawals of ~$41K/yr stay in low brackets and below OAS clawback. You save ~$290K in lifetime tax.

Keep contributing $18K/yr when:
  • ✓ Salary is $100K+ (deduction at 29-37% rate)
  • ✓ You plan retirement withdrawals under $90K/yr
  • ✓ You hold US-listed ETFs (0% withholding)
Stop contributing when:
  • ✗ Salary drops below $90K
  • ✗ RRSP balance exceeds $800K
  • ✗ You expect retirement income above $150K/yr

Why all swap ETFs? Why not Canadian dividend stocks?

Swap ETFs generate zero AAII (protecting your opco's SBD) and defer all tax until you sell. While Canadian dividends are 0% effective tax, they still require Part IV tax cash flow and add complexity. For simplicity: all swaps, buy and hold, CDA builds when you eventually sell.

Capital Dividend Account (CDA)

Every time you sell holdings at a gain, 50% goes to CDA. You can withdraw that amount 100% tax-free via capital dividend.

Do I need to force-sell every December?

No. The original plan had a complex annual sell/rebuy ritual. In practice, the CDA builds naturally over 15-25 years as you rebalance, take profits, or fund retirement. Don't force taxable events just to build CDA — let it happen organically. When you need cash, sell, and use the CDA portion tax-free.

Warning: Never declare a capital dividend exceeding your CDA balance. CRA charges a 60% penalty on the excess. Always confirm with your accountant first.

The $50K AAII Threshold

Passive investment income over $50K/yr grinds down your opco's Small Business Deduction. With an all-swap holdco portfolio: AAII = $0 until you sell. You have full control.
