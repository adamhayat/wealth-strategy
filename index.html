Critical Watch Items

  • ⚠ 21-Year Rule — each trust has a deemed disposition every 21 years from creation. Track these dates. Plan a rollout or new freeze before the deadline. Ask your trust lawyer.

Why NOT Max RRSP? The RRIF Trap.

At $31.5K/yr for 20 years, your RRSP hits ~$1.37M. At age 72, forced RRIF withdrawals of ~$72K+/year push you into the 43-53% bracket AND trigger full OAS clawback (starts at ~$93K income). You lose control.

At $14,958/yr (from $83.1K salary), the RRSP reaches ~$650K. RRIF withdrawals of ~$34K/yr stay in the 20-29% bracket — well below OAS clawback. Combined with CPP ($17K) and CDA dividends ($50K tax-free), your retirement income is ~$96K+ at a blended 15-20% tax rate. That's ~$350K saved in lifetime tax vs maxing RRSP.

Keep contributing ~$15K/yr when:
  • ✓ Salary is $83.1K (deduction saves ~30%)
  • ✓ RRSP balance is under $650K
  • ✓ You hold US-listed ETFs (0% withholding in RRSP)
Stop contributing when:
  • ✗ RRSP balance exceeds $650K
  • ✗ You expect retirement income above $90K/yr from other sources
  • ✗ Holdco CDA balance alone covers your withdrawal needs

Why 20% XIC? The GRIP Solution.

Without GRIP, all dividends to you are non-eligible (~3-5% more personal tax). XIC pays Canadian eligible dividends which the holdco receives at 0% effective tax (Part IV is 100% refundable) and generates GRIP so you can pay yourself eligible dividends at lower personal rates. On a $2M holdco, the 20% XIC position generates ~$12K/yr in Canadian dividends — enough GRIP for your dividend needs.

What about AAII from XIC dividends?

Canadian eligible dividends received by the holdco do NOT count toward AAII — they are excluded from the aggregate investment income calculation. Only foreign dividends, interest, and capital gains count. So XIC dividends are fully AAII-safe. The swap ETFs handle the rest.

Capital Dividend Account (CDA)

Every time you sell holdings at a gain, 50% goes to CDA. You can withdraw that amount 100% tax-free via capital dividend.

Do I need to force-sell every December?

No. The original plan had a complex annual sell/rebuy ritual. In practice, the CDA builds naturally over 15-25 years as you rebalance, take profits, or fund retirement. Don't force taxable events just to build CDA — let it happen organically. When you need cash, sell, and use the CDA portion tax-free.

Warning: Never declare a capital dividend exceeding your CDA balance. CRA charges a 60% penalty on the excess. Always confirm with your accountant first.

The $50K AAII Threshold

Passive investment income over $50K/yr grinds down your opco's Small Business Deduction. With an all-swap holdco portfolio: AAII = $0 until you sell. You have full control.
